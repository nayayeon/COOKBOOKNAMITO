Boneless Bangus

Ingredients:

  • 2 pieces of boneless bangus (milkfish), cleaned and prepped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon calamansi juice (or lemon juice as a substitute)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, calamansi juice, minced garlic, black pepper, and salt.
  2. Add the boneless bangus to the marinade, making sure it is well-coated.
  3. Cover and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. For better flavor, you can marinate it for a few hours or overnight.
  4. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  5. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the marinated bangus in the skillet.
  6. Cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until the fish is golden brown and crispy.
  7. Remove from the skillet and drain on paper towels.
  8. Serve the boneless bangus hot with steamed rice and your favorite side dishes. It pairs well with a simple tomato and onion salad or a dipping sauce made of vinegar and soy sauce.

Ginisang KangKong

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach), cleaned and trimmed
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian option)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup water or vegetable broth (optional, for more sauce)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Clean and trim the kangkong. Set aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat the cooking oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic, chopped onion, and chopped tomato. Sauté until the garlic is fragrant and the onion is translucent.
  3. Add KangKong: Add the kangkong to the pan. Stir and cook until the kangkong starts to wilt.
  4. Season: Add the fish sauce (or soy sauce) and stir well. If you prefer a bit more sauce, add water or vegetable broth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Cook Until Tender: Continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until the kangkong is tender and well-seasoned.
  6. Serve: Remove from heat and serve hot as a side dish with rice or as part of a meal.

Paksiw

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole bangus (milkfish), cleaned and cut into serving pieces
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced
  • 2-3 pieces of eggplant, sliced (optional)
  • 2-3 pieces of green chilies (siling haba)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis)
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (optional for color)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Clean and cut the bangus into serving pieces. Slice the vegetables and prepare the garlic and ginger.
  2. Assemble in Pot: In a pot, arrange the bangus slices. Add the garlic, onion, ginger, and green chilies. Place the eggplant slices on top if using.
  3. Add Liquid: Pour in the vinegar and water over the fish and season with fish sauce, soy sauce (optional), peppercorns, and a pinch of salt.
  4. Bring to a Boil: Turn on the heat and bring the mixture to a boil without stirring. Once it starts boiling, lower the heat to simmer.
  5. Simmer Until Cooked: Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until the fish is cooked through and the flavors have melded together. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve: Once done, serve the Paksiw na Bangus hot with steamed rice. Enjoy!

Papaitan

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg beef innards (intestines, liver, kidney), cleaned and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 kg beef shank, cut into chunks
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, minced
  • 1/4 cup bile extract (or 1 tablespoon bile concentrate, optional for authenticity)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 liter beef broth or water
  • 2-3 green chilies, sliced
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons bagoong (fermented shrimp paste, optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Add beef shank chunks and cook until browned.
  3. Pour in the beef broth or water, and bring to a boil. Lower the heat and simmer until the beef is tender, about 1-1.5 hours.
  4. Add the beef innards and continue to cook until tender, about 30 minutes.
  5. Stir in the bile extract (if using), fish sauce, ground black pepper, and green chilies. Adjust seasoning with salt and bagoong if desired.
  6. Simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes. Serve hot with steamed rice.
